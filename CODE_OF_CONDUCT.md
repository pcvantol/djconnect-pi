# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

DJConnect is a project for people who enjoy building thoughtful, useful music
experiences together. We want this community to be welcoming, respectful and
productive for contributors of all backgrounds and experience levels.

Everyone who participates in this project is expected to help create an
environment where people can ask questions, share ideas, review work and learn
without being dismissed, harassed or personally attacked.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful and considerate in issues, pull requests, reviews and
  discussions.
- Give feedback on the work, not on the person doing the work.
- Assume good intent when something is unclear, and ask clarifying questions
  before escalating tone.
- Welcome newcomers and help them understand the project conventions.
- Respect maintainers' decisions about scope, roadmap, safety and project
  direction.
- Keep discussions focused on DJConnect, its integrations, clients, docs and
  community needs.

## Unacceptable Behavior

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- Harassment, intimidation, threats or sustained disruption.
- Personal attacks, insults, slurs or demeaning language.
- Sexualized language or unwanted sexual attention.
- Discrimination based on identity, background, ability, age, religion,
  nationality, race, ethnicity, gender identity, sexual orientation or similar
  personal characteristics.
- Publishing private information, credentials, logs with secrets or other
  sensitive material without explicit permission.
- Repeatedly ignoring project boundaries after maintainers have clarified them.

## Project Boundaries

DJConnect spans Home Assistant integration code, Raspberry Pi clients, other
clients and shared product contracts. Community discussion should respect the
repository's scope and the documented cross-repository contract.

For this Raspberry Pi client repository in particular:

- Do not request or add microphone upload, push-to-talk, local DJ response
  audio or `/api/device/dj_response` behavior unless the product decision
  changes.
- Keep app updates, OS maintenance and the touch UI as separate processes.
- Keep secrets, private user data and sensitive diagnostics out of public
  issues and pull requests.

## Reporting

If you see or experience behavior that violates this code of conduct, contact a
project maintainer privately when possible. Include enough context for the
maintainer to understand what happened, such as links, screenshots or message
timestamps, while avoiding unnecessary disclosure of private information.

Maintainers may remove comments, close issues or pull requests, restrict
participation or take other reasonable action to protect the project community.

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying standards of acceptable behavior and
may take proportionate action when this code of conduct is violated. Enforcement
decisions should consider context, impact, prior behavior and the safety of the
community.

Possible actions include:

- A private clarification or warning.
- Editing or removing harmful content.
- Temporary limits on participation.
- Permanent removal from project spaces controlled by the maintainers.
